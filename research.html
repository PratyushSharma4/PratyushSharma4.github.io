Publications and Research Experience

Publications

  • 3SUM in Preprocessed Universes: Faster and Simpler — Shashwat Kasliwal, Adam Polak, Pratyush Sharma accepted at the SIAM Symposium on Simplicity in Algorithms 25, presents an efficient algorithm for solving a variant of 3SUM with preprocessing. Read the full paper on arXiv.

Research Projects

  • Research Internship with Prof. Adam Polak, Bocconi University (May 2024 - July 2024) — During this internship, I advanced the current bounds on the 3SUM variant problem, reducing it from O(N11/6) to O(N1.5 log(N)) by FFT (Fast Fourier Transform) and hashing techniques. Additionally, I worked on extending the solution to handle unknown set configurations in preprocessing and developed a deterministic approach to further enhance efficiency. Currently, I am exploring methods to improve the time complexity for the Dynamic Range Mode problem.
  • Research Project with Prof. Rajendra Kumar, IIT Delhi (August 2024 - Present) — This project focuses on the applications of the Continuous Learning with Errors (CLWE) problem from lattice-based cryptography. I am investigating how CLWE can enhance hardness assumptions in the realm of clustering algorithms, particularly for k-means clustering.
