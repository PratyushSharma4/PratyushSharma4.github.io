The Flaw in the Casino Game Betting Strategy

Question: What is the flaw in the casino game betting strategy of doubling your bet after every loss?
Answer: The flaw lies in applying the linearity of expectation without recognizing that the betting strategy leads to an infinite series of bets that do not converge. While each game has a fair expectation of $0, the doubling strategy can lead to a situation where losses accumulate exponentially, potentially leading to financial ruin before a win occurs.

Imagine a simple casino game with exactly a 50% chance of winning and a 50% chance of losing. The game sounds fair enough, but what if we decide to apply a particular betting strategy? Suppose I bet $1 and keep doubling my bet each time I lose until I eventually win. Assuming I have unlimited money, I would theoretically gain $1 as soon as I win the game. Since each game has an expectation of $0 due to the 50-50 win-loss split, it might seem like I should still walk away with a profit regardless of the expectation value being zero. So, where is the flaw in this reasoning?

The mistake lies in applying the linearity of expectation to this situation without the necessary conditions. Linearity of expectation works when we have either a finite set of random variables or an infinite series of random variables that converge absolutely. In this casino game setup, however, we're dealing with an infinite series of bets that do not converge — precisely because we are doubling our bet each time we lose.

Here's what happens: if I keep losing and doubling my bet indefinitely, the sum of my bets grows exponentially. Since I assume unlimited money, I am essentially betting an infinite amount over time, which fails the absolute convergence criterion needed for linearity of expectation. Without convergence, expectation cannot be applied as assumed, which invalidates the conclusion that I will walk away with profit.

In reality, resources are always limited, and even with very large funds, the probability of a long string of losses remains. Such a losing streak could lead to financial ruin before a win occurs. Therefore, while each individual game has a fair expectation of $0, trying to apply this expectation over an infinite, non-converging series of bets creates a flaw in the logic and an unrealistic view of “guaranteed profit.”
