The Flaw in the Casino Game Betting Strategy

Question: Imagine a simple casino game with exactly a 50% chance of winning and a 50% chance of losing. The game sounds fair enough, but what if we decide to apply a particular betting strategy? Suppose I bet $1 and keep doubling my bet each time I lose until I eventually win. Assuming I have unlimited money, I would theoretically gain $1 as soon as I win the game. Since each game has an expectation of $0 due to the 50-50 win-loss split, it might seem like I should still walk away with a profit regardless of the expectation value being zero. So, where is the flaw in this reasoning?
Answer: The mistake lies in applying the linearity of expectation to this situation without the necessary conditions. Linearity of expectation works when we have either a finite set of random variables or an infinite series of random variables that converge absolutely.
